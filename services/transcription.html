Verbatim Vs. Non-Verbatim



Verbatim transcription captures every single word spoken, including filler words ("um," "uh," "like"), false starts, stutters, repetitions, and non-verbal cues (e.g., "laughs," "sighs"). Verbatim aims to provide a complete and accurate record of the spoken word, reflecting the natural flow of conversation. Useful for legal proceedings, research interviews, qualitative analysis, and other cases where precise language and delivery are critical. Verbatim projects are therefore more expensive to transcribe.

Non-verbatim transcription removes filler words, false starts, and repetitions, creating a cleaner and more readable transcript. It focuses on conveying the essential meaning of the spoken word while omitting unnecessary disfluencies. Non-verbal cues are usually omitted unless they are vital to the meaning. Compared to verbatim projects, non-verbaim projects are easier to transcribe.
